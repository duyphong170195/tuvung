unwind /ʌnˈwaɪnd/ (v)
1. thư giãn (to begin to relax after you have been working hard or feeling nervous)

2. tháo, cởi (to unfasten something that is wrapped around an object)
unwind - unwound - unwound
1.
  • Music helps me unwind after a busy day.

    • 2.
  • He unwound his scarf from his neck.
  • In a nearby medical tent, a US Army doctor gently unwinds Metruk's bandage.
    		•
    recuperate /rɪˈkuː.pəreɪt/ (v) hồi phục (to get back your health, strength or energy after being ill, tired, injured, etc.)
    (syn) recover
  • She's going to need time to recuperate after the operation.
  • Hopefully Jean will recuperate quickly and be able to leave the hospital soon.
    		•
    snowboarding /ˈsnoʊ.bɔːrdɪŋ/ (n) trượt tuyết (the activity or sport of moving over snow using a snowboard)
  • Snowboarding became an Olympic sport in 1998.
    		•
    second to none /nʌn/ (phrase) là nhất, anh là số hai không ai số một (the best, better than all others)
  • These days, Apple’s reputation for innovation is second to none.
  • Mom's chocolate cake is second to none.
  • The desserts here are truly second to none.
    		•
    cuisine /kwɪˈziːn/ (n) ẩm thực (a style of cooking, generally refers to food as a category from a certain culture)
  • cuisine
  • Melanie’s dream was to travel the world and experience a variety of cultural cuisine.
    		•
    doctorate /ˈdɒk.tərət/ (n) học vị tiến sĩ (the highest academic degree given by a university)
  • She earned a doctorate in political sociology at City University of New York.

    brokerage /ˈbroʊ.kərɪdʒ/ (n)
  • lucrative /ˈluː.krə.t̬ɪv/ (adj) sinh nhiều lợi nhuận
    (especially of a business, job, or activity) producing a lot of money
  • lucrative
  • The firm has a lucrative business contract with the Scottish Executive.
  • He inherited a lucrative business from his father.
    		•
    on behalf of someone (phrase)
    1. thay mặt cho (as someone's agent or representative)

    2. cho lợi ích của ai đó (for the benefit, advantage, or interest of someone)
    also in behalf of someone
    1.
  • Mr Knight cannot be here, so his wife will accept the prize on his behalf.
  • In behalf of the board, I want to thank you for your help.

    • 2.
  • Children don't understand that everything their parents do is in behalf of them.
  • The foundation raised more than $250,000 in behalf of refugees of foreign wars.
